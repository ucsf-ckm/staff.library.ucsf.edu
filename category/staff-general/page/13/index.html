You’ve probably heard of TED talks and TEDMED…

April 12, 2013 by Julia Kochi

You’ve probably heard of TED talks, and TEDMED follows a similar vein but with an emphasis on multidisciplinary thinking in health, medicine and science. This year’s TEDMED is taking place April 16 – 19, and Scientific American is providing free access to its institutional subscribers.

If you’re interested in watching some of the sessions, point your browser to http://www.livestream.co/tedmed . Once there, choose University of California, San Francisco and use the Affiliate ID L29124 or L44414. You can watch in real-time or on demand.

Chancellor Susan Desmond-Hellmann is speaking on Thursday between 8am – 9:45am. You can see a preliminary schedule at http://www.tedmed.com/event/abouttheevent?ref=schedule .

Take some time to view some talks. You’ll be inspired!

My UCSF Internship: Spencer Wright

April 30, 2014December 12, 2012 by Polina Ilieva

“For this past fall, I’ve had the pleasure of working for UCSF in their archives. The projects that I’ve had the privilege of working on include the Google books digitization project, helping organize and catalogue DVDs and VHS tapes related to the Legacy Tobacco Documents Library, along with helping find other academic archives to augment UCSF’s own HIV/AIDS collection. As someone planning to become a librarian, I found these experiences immensely rewarding.
Perhaps my favorite project out of these was the Google books digitization project. While many people would hesitate at the thought of wandering through shelves of old books for several hours on end being an enjoyable experience, I found it to be wonderful. I enjoyed the opportunity to spend all that time roaming the shelves and going through all the books and texts, getting excited every time I recognized a certain title or author (though admittedly, most of the books I recognized were largely limited to the works of Freud and Jung).
My internship this past fall at UCSF has been wonderful.”

Posted on behalf of Spencer Wright.
