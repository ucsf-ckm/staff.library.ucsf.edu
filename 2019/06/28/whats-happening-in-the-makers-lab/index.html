What’s Happening in the Makers Lab

June 28, 2019 by Dylan Romero

The Makers Lab opened three years ago and the community of makers at UCSF continues to grow. We know everyone is busy working away in the Library, so I want to take a moment to share a few updates. As a reminder, here is an interview discussing the role of library makerspaces – spoiler alert, we totally agree! 😉

Here are some stats from 2018:

  • 6,330 Makers Lab visits
  • 187 pop-ups (43 unique topics covered at the pop-ups from knitting to 3D printing!)
  • Partnered with the UCSF Department of Anatomy to offer Anatomy 170.10 Introduction to 3D Printing for Health Science Students in the fall and spring
  • 5,068+ hours of 3D printing

The future is bright and we look forward to expanding our services to support VR and other XR technologies in the coming year. We encourage Library staff to use the Makers Lab; it is your makerspace! The opportunities are endless – you can use the sewing machine to repair an old pair of jeans or check out a Raspberry Pi to work on a Python project. Susan Boone and Haley McEwan recently shared their needlework skills with School of Dentistry post-bac students, teaching them lessons in dexterity and creativity.

And Wes Cayabyab from the Kanbar Simulation Center stopped by in June to lead a DIY silicone suturing pad making session for medicine and nursing students.

Here are a few ways Library staff can get involved:

  1. Reserve equipment anytime the Makers Lab is open
  2. Suggest a pop-up or event – you can find some examples here
  3. Become a volunteer and staff for 2-4 hours per month (speak with your manager first)
  4. Visit! If you need a mental break from your daily routine drop-in during our open hours

I personally love to spend time in the Makers Lab because it is an opportunity to connect with people from all across UCSF. We provide tools and events that foster creativity, something that the UCSF community craves. This helps to prevent burnout and empower people to be their best while at school, in the clinic, or lab.

As a reminder, the Makers Lab is open Monday-Friday, 10am-6pm. Learn more on the Makers Lab website, follow us on Instagram @ucsfmakerslab, or come up and chat!
