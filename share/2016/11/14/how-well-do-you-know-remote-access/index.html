How Well Do You Know Remote Access?

November 14, 2016November 14, 2016 by Richard Trott

Pop quiz time!

A UCSF faculty member is on vacation in Maui. She receives an email from a researcher at NYU. The email has a link to an article in an Elsevier publication. She clicks on the link but can’t get the full text. She remembers, “Oh, right! I’m not on a UCSF network right now!” She types myaccess.ucsf.edu into her browser location bar, logs in, and then goes back to the email to click the link again. Does she get the full text of the article?

  1. Yes because she now has a UCSF IP address and appears to be on the UCSF network.
  2. Yes because she is now on the VPN.
  3. Yes because by logging into myaccess.ucsf.edu, she is now going through EZproxy whenever she goes to the Elsevier site.
  4. No because obviously this is a trick question and the answer is “no”.

Keep reading for the answer…

…which is no, she does not get the full text.

Here’s the important thing to remember:

Going directly to myaccess.ucsf.edu will not give you full-text access.

Ever been to one of those sites that lets you log in, but instead of creating an account for the site specifically, it lets you log in with Facebook or Twitter or your Google account? Think of MyAccess that way.

If you use your Facebook account to access AmazingCatPhotos.com™, you don’t login to Facebook and then go to AmazingCatPhotos.com™. You go to AmazingCatPhotos.com™ and click a link or button that says “Log in with Facebook”.

EZproxy works very similarly. When you’re on an article or resource page, clicking the “Reload via EZproxy” browser button prompts you to login to MyAccess and then takes you back to the full-text.

The key is that if EZproxy sends you to MyAccess, then you’re all set. But if you go directly to MyAccess, it won’t give you full access.

Remote access is confusing, and much of the confusion is not easily remedied. Equipping Library staff and users with accurate information is the only way (for now, anyway) to reduce the frustrations of remote access. Please feel free to leave any questions or comments here, or in Slack, or in email. Thanks for reading!
