User Research Results Part 2: Library News

September 29, 2020 by Michele Mizejewski

News posts and newsletter content enable the library to share current happenings and to highlight and promote services. Our goal is to make these types of information discoverable in multiple places and whenever possible, put them into the path of the people who will find them most useful. Some users come to our home page, while others only visit selected areas that fall within their personal work and focus.

What did users have to say?

When tasked with locating news on a particular subject, testers tended to scan the website’s top navigation for this information.

“I’m mostly just scanning for if there’s news.” 

“I wouldn’t scroll to the very bottom of the page to see that.”

Testing also revealed that most users went to the general All News page (see below) to find posts on a particular subject rather than first seeking a more subject-specific page (e.g. Scholarly Communication). 

But when users arrived at the All News webpage, they tended to do a lot of scanning and scrolling to locate the information they were seeking, which was frustrating to some. 

“If there was a list of topics at the top to choose from, it would help. Otherwise, I might just leave the page.”

Users also had difficulty signing up for library newsletters, because they didn’t see the button in the footer, or they expected to see a way to sign up on the All News webpage.

“For newsletters, I usually go to the bottom, I don’t know why. There’s nothing there.”

“I’d go to your news section and see if there is a way to subscribe and I don’t see one.”

What’s new?

To make it more intuitive for users to find news on particular topics and to sign up for selected newsletters, we’ve made several improvements.

News and events in the top navigation

Based on the user expectation to find news and other current information in the top level navigation, we have added a new menu for News and Events. Those things are still integrated into more specific pages like The Makers Lab, for example, but the menu offers another highly visible way for users to reach this core information about current library happenings and services.

New options on the All News page

The All News page is now more easily scanned and less overwhelming. In addition to a visual list of posts in all areas, it includes a field to search for news posts and also offers a list of topics (a.k.a. categories) by which to filter the content. Users can also subscribe to curated newsletters right from this page.

News in specific areas

Those same categories are still used to insert the latest relevant news into specific pages like Instructional Design or Data Science, so that users who go straight to those areas will find related news right in their path.

News on the home page

The latest three posts appear on the home page along with a badge indicating their topic/category. The style of the badges was adjusted, so they can no longer be confused with the dark blue action buttons that are used across the site.

Subscribing to curated newsletters

Although there was already a way to subscribe to newsletters in the footer, user testing revealed that it was often missed. In response, that call-to-action button as well as links to social media were moved into the main footer and the white bar was eliminated. This results in increased visibility while remaining in a conventional location.

In addition, the descriptions for those newsletters were updated in collaboration with Joanna to make it more clear to users what they can expect and why they should subscribe.

The Help Center

We also wanted to be sure that if a user looks for a way to subscribe to newsletters in the Help Center that they will be led in the right direction. To address that, there is now a section called News and Events and results will be surfaced when searching the Help Center or its Help widget that appears at the bottom of all pages on library.ucsf.edu.

Conclusion

These changes directly address some of the pain points raised by users during the recent usability testing. As always, we will continue to observe user feedback and iterate as needed. Keep an eye out for future posts on website improvements based on user feedback. Bring your questions or comments to Slack (#ux-service), email us, or leave a comment below.

This post is one in a series that describe user experience improvements based on user research performed in June and July 2020.
